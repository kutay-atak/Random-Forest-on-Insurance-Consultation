What Drives Expected Loss

Effect of each factor on expected loss, all else held equal. Numeric features show the impact of a one standard-deviation increase. Reference: Female · Employed · CarType A · Cover 1 · Year 2009.

Impact on Expected Loss (%)
Right of zero = increases loss · Left of zero = decreases loss
🔴 Unemployment compounds severely Being unemployed adds +35% expected loss vs employed customers. Combined with youth, it drives some customers to 4–5× the portfolio average.
🔴 Urban density is the hidden driver A one-SD rise in area density raises expected loss by +32%. Dense postcodes correlate with both higher frequency and severity.
🔴 Gender and cover gaps Male policyholders carry +24% over female. Customers without cover (cover=0) add a further +20% — both are actionable at underwriting.
🟢 Age is the strongest protector Each additional year of age reduces expected loss by ~2.4%. A 50-year-old is roughly 47% lower-risk than a 25-year-old, all else equal.
🟢 Tenure rewards loyalty Each year with AEGAS reduces expected loss by ~1.6%. Long-tenured customers self-select into lower risk — factor into retention pricing.
🟡 Car type plays a secondary role CarType D reduces loss by 9% vs the riskier Type A reference. Car value matters less than demographic factors (+3.7% per SD).
